The Young Gentleman's Book (Containing a Series of Choice Readings in Popular Science and Natural History)

1834 (Second Edition)

The Young Gentleman's Book Containing a Series of Choice Lectures in Popular History and Natural History, Together with Retrospective Essays, Conversations, Literary Reminiscences, Etc. (Second Edition, 1834) is the oldest book in my collection (so far), and definitely the catalyst for my obsession with 1800s volumes - it doesn't quite fit with what I usually collect, but that beautiful marbling convinced me to buy it.
Covering a vast range of topics (and I mean that in the most literal sense), this guidebook introduces topics in geology, astronomy, biology, chemistry and physics, aimed at "young gentlemen".
Possibly it's most unique feature is a chapter covering over 150 "Conversational Topics", covering everything from the Aurora Borealis to stilton cheese (which can conveniently be found on the same page). [include quote about women and books]

London : Baldwin and Cradock, 1834 (second ed.).
Hardcover : marbled endpapers : a.e.m. : leather boards with marbled cover : five raised bands to spine with title lettered in gilt in second compartment and guilt pattern to other five compartments : noticable wear all over.

Handbook for "young gentlemen" discussing various topics on science and natural history. Fair condition.
