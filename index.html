My Collection

About

Hi! My name is Natalie and I am a 20-year-old science student and educator from Sydney, Australia. I have been interested in collecting books since high school, where I fell in love with the 1980s Coroneos textbook my Year 11 maths teacher showed us - something about the typewritten pages littered with hand-drawn diagrams was so much more appealing than the computer-generated textbooks I had for all my other subjects.
My collection currently comprises around thirty books on various STEM topics, predominantly astronomy and physics. I collect these areas as they align with what I am currently studying in my third year of my Bachelor of Science. I find it invaluable to have access to the history of the modern concepts I hear about in my lectures, as I believe science is at its most valuable when we acknowledge how it has developed over time. To quote my 2023 entry to the Young Book Collector's Prize:

"I collect books to preserve knowledge and use it to inform both myself and others. When my astrophysics homework leaves me lost, I have hundreds of years of background information to set me on the right path. When my young students bombard me with “why” in response to whatever answer I give them, I am prepared to take them on a journey through decades of technological advancement. When the world is faced with uncertainty, I know I have the voices of thousands of academics, scientists, and researchers preserved in ink, and an internet connection through which to share it."

I hope you enjoy learning about my collection - if you have any questions, you can contact me through @astronat__ on Instagram :)
